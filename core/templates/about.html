Our Heritage

The Pride of Abia State

Bende Local Government Area is one of the oldest and most historically significant LGAs in Abia State. Known for its rich agricultural soil, vibrant cultural festivals, and peaceful disposition, Bende stands as a pillar of economic and social development in the region.

Our mission is to foster a united community through transparent governance, infrastructural development, and the empowerment of our people. From the rice fields of Ozuitem to the bustling markets of Uzuakoli, Bende is a land of opportunity.

Our Communities

Discover the towns and villages that make up the vibrant tapestry of Bende Local Government Area.

Bende Town

The administrative headquarters of the LGA. Bende Town is the seat of local governance and home to the historic Council Secretariat.

Uzuakoli

Famous for its historic slave trade route history, the Methodist Institute, and one of the largest markets in the region.

Igbere

A prominent community known for its entrepreneurial spirit, scenic hills, and significant contributions to media and business.

Ozuitem

Often referred to as the food basket of Bende, Ozuitem is renowned for large-scale agriculture, particularly rice and cassava.

Item

Rich in culture and war dance history. The Item community is a cluster of villages with a strong communal bond.

Alayi

Known for its breathtaking landscape and hills. Alayi is a peaceful community with a growing educational sector.

Itumbauzo

Located near the border, Itumbauzo is key for cross-border trade and possesses vast arable land for farming.

Ugwueke

A serene community known for its hospitality and palm oil production. It plays a vital role in the local economy.

Umuhu-Ezechi

A strategic community bordering the capital territory, known for its rapid development and welcoming people.

Bende LGA

Modernizing governance through technology. We are committed to transparency and development.
